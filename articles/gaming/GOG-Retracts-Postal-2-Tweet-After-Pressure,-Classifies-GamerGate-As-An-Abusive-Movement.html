GOG Retracts Postal 2 Tweet After Pressure, Classifies GamerGate As An "Abusive Movement"

20th of July 2018 by TheChiefMeat

GOG recently advertised the newly released Postal 2: Paradise Lost for their online distribution store, prompting some users to fire backlash back at GOG. The tweet in question shows a gravestone noting the date of the now infamous day that multiple gaming press outlets simultaneously published "Gamers Are Dead" articles, which helped give rise to the GamerGate movement which seeked to restore ethical journalistic policies for said outlets.



After various Twitter users fired backlash towards GOG, GOG pulled the tweet in question and issued the below apology, sparking even more backlash from GamerGate supporters over GOGs apparent lack of understanding about the movement, and the mischaracterisation of the movement in general:

We'd like to follow up on our recent tweet about Postal 2 to share our apology and a few thoughts:

— GOG.COM (@GOGcom) July 19, 2018

• The intention behind our tweet was to inform about a release known for controversial content.
• Unfortunately, we've failed to make the association between the image, the date, and an abusive movement.
• Our intention was never to hurt or condone hate.

— GOG.COM (@GOGcom) July 19, 2018

We have removed the tweet now, we clearly messed up here.

— GOG.COM (@GOGcom) July 19, 2018

GOG has yet to respond to the open criticism it has faced from fans.
