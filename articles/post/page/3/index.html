Australian Senate Bill to Stop Bans on Gaming

July 18, 2018
Gaming News
Australian senator David Leyonhjelm has introduced the Freedom of Speech Legislation Amendment proposal, which would amend the Classification (Publications, Films and Computer Games) Act 1995 to: Remove the ban on publications, films and computer games that offend against standards of morality, decency and propriety. And also amend the Broadcasting Services Act 1992 to: Remove bans on broadcasting, datacasting and online content, with a specific focus on bans affecting services provided behind paywalls; narrow the guidance provided by government to broadcasting industries and datacasting licensees in the development of codes of practice; remove certain restrictions on subscription television broadcasters and online content services relating to programs or content that has been or would be classified as X 18+, ‘category 1 restricted’ or ‘category 2 restricted’; and remove a ban on broadcasting electoral advertising relating to a federal, state, territory or local election on election day or on the preceding Thursday or Friday.

Germany Bans Pre Orders Without Set Release Dates

July 16, 2018
Gaming News
The Higher Regional Court of Munich has ruled that pre-orders with “vague” release dates are illegal. The issue was brought to the regional court by a German rights advocacy group, who sued Mediamarkt for illegal information in the online ordering of a smartphone. According to the judgement, indeterminate statements regarding the delivery of online goods and orders violates the legal duty of information of the seller. The customer is entitled to a specific delivery date before clicking on the order button, and information such as “soon to be available” are in violation of the consumers rights.
